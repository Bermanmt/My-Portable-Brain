🧠 Portable Brain

Set up your AI-powered personal vault in a few minutes.

Welcome

What is this?

You're about to create a Brain Vault — a personal knowledge system that any AI can read instantly. It gives your AI agent context about who you are, what you're working on, and how you like to work.
Here's what happens:
1. You'll answer a few questions about yourself and your goals
2. You'll set up your AI agent's personality
3. You'll download a config file
4. Run one command to create your vault

Takes about 5 minutes. You can change everything later.
Step 1 of 6

About you

Your agent needs to know who you are. This info goes into USER.md — a file your AI reads at the start of every session so it doesn't ask you the same questions twice.
One line — your role or what describes you best right now.
Step 2 of 6

How you work

This helps your agent match your style. It won't overload you with information if you prefer concise answers, and it won't skip details if you like thoroughness.
How should your agent talk to you? Think about what annoys you in conversations with AI.
What tools do you use daily? This helps the agent give relevant advice.
Things that affect how you work — the agent remembers these permanently.
One rule that guides how you approach work. The agent uses this to filter suggestions.
Step 3 of 6

Your life roles

Roles are the most important input in the whole system. Every time you or your agent consider taking on a new commitment, it gets checked against these roles. If it doesn't serve a role, it probably shouldn't be on your plate.
Think: what are the hats you wear in life?
Examples: Founder, Father, Partner, Builder, Friend, Athlete
Aim for 4–7. Too many means no real priorities.
One role per line. These go into your Core System and guide everything else.
Step 4 of 6

Your AI agent

Your agent has a name, a personality, and rules about what it should never do. This goes into SOUL.md — the file that shapes how your agent thinks and talks. Pick something that feels right. You can change it anytime.
How would you describe this agent to a friend? One sentence.
How should the agent sound when talking to you?
Hard rules. The agent will follow these strictly.
Step 5 of 6

Your core system

This is the foundation layer — the big-picture stuff that everything else connects to. Your themes shape the year. Your Misogi is the one challenge that defines it. These go into 08-CoreSystem and your yearly planning note.
3 words or phrases that define what this year is about for you.
One big, scary challenge for the year — something with a real chance of failure. The Japanese concept of a defining test.
If you have something active, we'll create a project folder for it.
Step 6 of 6

Review & download

Here's everything you entered. Check it looks right, then download your config file.
# After downloading, open your terminal and run:
$ bash onboard.sh --config brain-config.json

# Or specify where to create your vault:
$ bash onboard.sh --config brain-config.json --vault ~/Brain
What happens next?
The setup script reads your config and creates your vault — all the folders, templates, and automation files. Your first daily note will be waiting for you. Open the vault in your favorite markdown editor (Obsidian, VS Code, etc.) and start your first session with your agent.
