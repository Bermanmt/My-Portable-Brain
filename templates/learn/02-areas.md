# 02-Areas — What This Is

Areas are the parts of your life you're always maintaining.

Unlike projects, areas don't finish. Health doesn't complete. Your finances don't have a done state. Your career is always ongoing. These are responsibilities with a standard to uphold — not a finish line to cross.

## Projects vs Areas — the real difference

| | Projects | Areas |
|---|---------|-------|
| End state | Yes — specific, observable | No — ongoing |
| Example | "Launch v2 by April" | "Career" |
| Done? | Eventually | Never |
| Lives in | 01-Projects/ | 02-Areas/ |

Here's the useful relationship: **projects serve areas**. "Run a 5K" is a project that serves your Health area. "Ship Q2 features" is a project that serves your Career area. Areas give projects meaning. Projects move areas forward.

## What's in an area folder

Each area has an `index.md` that captures:
- The standard — what does "good enough" look like here?
- Active projects serving this area
- Relevant resources
- Goals linked to this area

You rarely write inside area files — mostly you link *to* them from projects and goals.

## Default areas

The vault starts with: `health/`, `finances/`, `career/`, `home/`. These are a starting point, not a prescription. Add areas that match your life, remove ones that don't apply.

## The standard

The most important thing to write in each area index is the standard. Not goals — the floor. "For health, good enough means: I exercise 3x per week, I sleep 7+ hours, and I'm not ignoring doctor visits." That's what you're maintaining. Projects push you above the floor.
