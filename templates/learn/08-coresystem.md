# 08-CoreSystem — What This Is

**The foundation. The reason everything else exists.**

Most productivity systems fail because they organize tasks without knowing
what the tasks are for. CoreSystem is the answer to "what is all of this for?"

Your agent reads these files before making suggestions about your time,
priorities, or decisions. A task that doesn't connect to any of your roles
gets flagged — not just added to a list.

---

## The Files

**`roles.md`** — The most important file in the vault.
Your current life roles. Everything in the vault should serve at least one.
Aim for 4–7. More than 7 means you have no real priorities.

**`principles.md`** — How you want to live and work.
Not goals. Not habits. The underlying operating principles you make decisions from.
Read these when you're stuck on a hard decision.

**`my-process.md`** — How you actually work.
Your real rhythm, not your ideal one. What gives you energy. What drains you.
How you make decisions. Your agent uses this to calibrate suggestions.

**`misogi-and-adventures.md`** — The one hard thing.
A Misogi is a yearly challenge with a real chance of failure. Not a goal — a test.
Mini adventures are smaller things worth doing. Experiences over achievements.

**`relationship-values.md`** — How you want to show up with people.
Not a contact list. The values that govern your relationships.

**`home-values.md`** — What home stands for.
The atmosphere, routines, and culture of your household.

---

## How Often to Update

- `roles.md` + `principles.md` → once per quarter, during quarterly planning
- `misogi-and-adventures.md` → once per year
- `my-process.md` → whenever something keeps not working
- The rest → when something significant changes

---

## This Is Not a To-Do List

Nothing in CoreSystem is actionable. It's all foundational.
When you're overwhelmed, come here — not to add tasks, but to remember
what actually matters and filter everything else through that lens.

---

## Start Here

If you haven't filled these in yet, start with `roles.md`.
Five minutes of honest writing here will save you hours of wrong priorities later.
